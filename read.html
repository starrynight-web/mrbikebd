Yamaha Launches New FZ-X 2025 in Bangladesh

June 17, 2025
Mr Bike BD Team
5 min read
2.4k views
Share:
The all-new Yamaha FZ-X 2025 with retro-modern design

Yamaha has officially launched the much-anticipated FZ-X 2025 in the Bangladeshi market, bringing a perfect blend of retro-inspired design with modern-day features and technology.

Key Highlights

  • 149cc air-cooled engine with 12.4 PS power
  • Retro-modern design with LED lighting
  • Bluetooth connectivity and digital console
  • Expected price: BDT 3,00,000 (ex-showroom)
  • Bookings open across major cities

Powerful Performance

The Yamaha FZ-X 2025 is powered by a reliable 149cc air-cooled, single-cylinder engine that produces 12.4 PS of power at 7,250 rpm and 13.3 Nm of torque at 5,500 rpm. The engine is mated to a smooth-shifting 5-speed gearbox that offers crisp gear changes and good highway cruising capabilities.

"This is a game-changer for those who want a mix of classic styling and modern tech. The youth demographic will absolutely love this motorcycle."

- Mr. Arefin, Yamaha Dealer, Dhaka

Design & Features

Yamaha has taken a bold step with the FZ-X's design language. The retro-inspired circular headlamp (with LED lighting), teardrop fuel tank, and flat seat blend perfectly with modern elements like the muscular fuel tank extensions, LED tail lamp, and alloy wheels.

The fully digital instrument cluster comes with Bluetooth connectivity through Yamaha's Y-Connect app, allowing riders to get call/SMS alerts, phone battery status, and other notifications directly on the console. The bike also features a side-stand engine cutoff and USB charging port.

Technical Specifications

Specification Details
Engine Type 149cc, air-cooled, 4-stroke, SOHC
Maximum Power 12.4 PS @ 7,250 rpm
Maximum Torque 13.3 Nm @ 5,500 rpm
Transmission 5-speed constant mesh
Brakes Front disc, rear drum (ABS variant available)
Fuel Tank Capacity 10 liters
Kerb Weight 139 kg

Pricing & Availability

The Yamaha FZ-X 2025 is expected to be priced around BDT 3,00,000 (ex-showroom). Bookings have already started at authorized Yamaha dealerships across major cities including Dhaka, Chittagong, Rajshahi, Khulna, and Sylhet. The first deliveries are expected to begin by the end of June 2025.

First Ride Experience

In our preliminary test ride, the FZ-X impressed with its comfortable upright riding posture, smooth city performance, and surprisingly good low-end torque. The suspension setup handles Bangladeshi road conditions well, absorbing potholes and speed breakers with ease. The bike feels planted at city speeds and stable on highways up to 100 km/h.

Yamaha claims the FZ-X 2025 is specifically designed for urban riders who seek both comfort and style. The seating posture is upright, reducing fatigue on longer commutes, and the suspension setup is tuned specifically for city conditions with frequent potholes and speed breakers.
